Services Page — Round 2

Three award-level directions inspired by Lusion, Linear, and David Langarica. Each leverages your GSAP stack and metro identity. Click to select.

Research insights: Award-winning services pages prove capabilities through storytelling, not lists. Lusion shows services through featured work. Linear uses granular type hierarchy with animated grid patterns. David Langarica uses color-coded cards with cycling role animations.

Your assets: 6 service SVGs, GSAP (SplitText, MorphSVGPlugin, ScrollTrigger, DrawSVGPlugin), Inter + JetBrains Mono, dark theme, orange/yellow accent, metro metaphor.
A
The Kinetic Scroll — Full-Viewport Service Reveals
immersive / lusion-inspired

Each service occupies the full viewport. As you scroll, the service SVG draws itself in (DrawSVGPlugin), the title splits and animates in with SplitText, and a brief value prop fades up. Scroll snapping locks each service. A thin vertical metro line on the left tracks progress with station dots that fill as you pass each service. The bottom of each viewport shows a "proof strip" — mini project thumbnails that link to /work/[slug]. Clicking the service title or a "deep dive" CTA navigates to /services/[id].

B
The Editorial Grid — Magazine-Style Asymmetry
editorial / linear-inspired

Think editorial magazine layout. A bold hero with oversized "What I Build" text (SplitText reveal). Below, services are presented in an asymmetric 2-column grid where items alternate between large and small. The first 2 services (SQL + Pipeline — your bread and butter) get large hero cards with the SVG illustration, full description, stack, and project count. The remaining 4 get compact editorial rows — title, one-liner, station number. All cards reveal with staggered use:reveal. Hover on any card shows a MorphSVG transition on the service icon. No scroll snapping — natural scroll with sticky section headers.

C
The Data Flow — Horizontal Scroll Service Map
interactive / skills-journey-inspired

Reuse the horizontal scroll pattern from SkillsJourney (Slice B) but for services. A hero section with "What I Build" drops into a horizontal scroll container where each service is a full-width panel. Each panel: large SVG animation (DrawSVGPlugin), title with SplitText animation, brief value prop, stack chips, and linked project mini-cards. A progress bar at the top shows "Service 01/06" advancing horizontally. The metro line runs horizontally through all panels, connecting them as a data flow. After the horizontal scroll, a vertical CTA section: "See these in action" linking to /work.
