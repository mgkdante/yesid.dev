## Recommended sequence

1. Before any campaign post, complete and save the strict Google/AI baseline.
2. Confirm all 18 routes, reciprocal locale switches, canonical/hreflang clusters, sitemap entries, OG metadata, mobile layout, and analytics.
3. Publish six LinkedIn posts over three weeks, with at least 72 hours between posts:
   - Lead-language order: Post 1 led in English; Post 2 led in Spanish; Post 3 led in French; Post 4 led in English; Post 5 led in Spanish; Post 6 led in French.
   - Week 1: Posts 1 and 2.
   - Week 2: Posts 3 and 4.
   - Week 3: Posts 5 and 6.
4. Each LinkedIn post includes all three clean article links. Use one lead-language draft, not all three versions on consecutive days.
5. Use the two non-lead short variants for relevant direct outreach or a later repost. Do not mass-post identical text across every profile.
6. Add UTM parameters only at publication time so the clean canonical links remain the review source. Use `utm_source=<channel>`, `utm_medium=social`, `utm_campaign=blog_launch_2026`, and `utm_content=article_<n>_<locale>`.
7. After seven days, compare visits, engaged time, outbound clicks, contact conversions, and booking clicks by article. Pin or feature the strongest link based on observed results, not preference.
