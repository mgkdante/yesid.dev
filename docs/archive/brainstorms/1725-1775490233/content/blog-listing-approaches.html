A — Editorial Hero + Grid

Latest post gets a big featured card (hero zone). Remaining posts in a 2-column grid below. Metro line runs vertically with station numbers. Magazine feel.

Pros

  • Strong visual hierarchy
  • Featured post gets spotlight
  • Familiar editorial pattern

Cons

  • Grid on mobile collapses to list anyway
  • More complex responsive behavior

B — Metro Timeline List

Posts as stops on a vertical metro line. Big typography, generous spacing, no cards — just text breathing. Station numbers anchor each post. Typographic + transit-native.

Pros

  • Transit brand IS the layout
  • Clean, scannable, reading-first
  • Mobile-first by nature

Cons

  • Less visual variety
  • No featured post hierarchy

C — Hybrid: Featured Stop + Timeline

Latest post is a highlighted "station card" with border accent + excerpt. Older posts are clean timeline rows. Metro line connects everything. Best of A + B.

Pros

  • Featured post gets spotlight
  • Metro timeline for wayfinding
  • Scales well on mobile

Cons

  • Slightly more complex than pure list

💡 My recommendation: C — gives you the editorial spotlight of A with the transit-native feel of B. The featured card creates hierarchy, and the metro line makes the blog feel like part of the yesid. journey.
