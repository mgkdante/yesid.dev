Question 1: Text Size & Layout

The manifesto is one sentence on a dark (#141414) full-viewport canvas. How big and how arranged?

I build the infrastructure
your operations run on.

A — Centered, 2 lines

Clean break after "infrastructure". ~clamp(2rem, 5vw, 4.5rem) on desktop. Classic Awwwards manifesto. Vertically centered in viewport.

Each phrase on its own line, left-aligned. Small label above ("What I do"). More editorial/magazine feel. Text starts left ~10% from edge.

C — Split with orange divider

Sentence split at the midpoint with a vertical orange line. Left side = what you do, right side = who benefits. Dramatic but might feel forced.

D — Oversized uppercase

All caps, word "INFRASTRUCTURE" even larger than the rest. Orange dot on the period. Bold, confrontational, maximum impact. Matches the hero's uppercase energy.

Animation behavior (applies to all layouts)

GSAP SplitText splits the sentence into individual characters. As the user scrolls through this section, characters reveal one by one (opacity 0 → 1, slight y-translate up). Orange words shift to brand color only after fully revealed. The reveal is scrubbed to scroll position — scroll back and chars disappear again.

On mobile, the text scales down proportionally with clamp() but keeps the same layout structure.
