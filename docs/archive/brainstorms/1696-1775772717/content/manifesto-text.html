Manifesto Section — Text & Tone

The manifesto is a single bold statement that bridges the hero into the rest of the page. It should feel like a confident declaration — not a tagline, not a sales pitch.

Context

The hero ends with the metro SVG animation and "DIGITAL INFRA BUILT RIGHT." The manifesto appears as the user scrolls past the hero — a full-viewport moment of pause before the proof sections begin. The text reveals character by character via GSAP SplitText as the user scrolls.

A

"I build the infrastructure your operations run on."

Direct, personal ("I build"), positions you as the hands-on builder. "Operations" signals enterprise/production systems. Short, punchy. From the spec.

B

"I build the systems that keep yours running."

More conversational. "Systems" is broader than "infrastructure" — captures web apps too. "Keep yours running" implies reliability and operational continuity. Slightly warmer tone.

C

"From pipeline to dashboard — I build what moves your data."

More specific and technical. Mentions the two endpoints of your work (ingest → visualization). "Moves your data" is active and kinetic. Longer — more characters for the SplitText reveal to work with.

D

Something else

You have a different idea or want to riff on one of these. Tell me in the terminal.
