3 Design Approaches for yesid.

Same content sections, different levels of ambition. Pick the direction that excites you.

A

Metro System Evolved (Recommended)

Enhance what exists. The current rail + train + station cards get bolder: thicker track, particle bursts at stations, glowing train trail. New sections (projects, about bento, blog, CTA) slot in as new "stops" on the line. Background gets animated data-flow particles. Typography gets bigger and bolder.

Effort: Medium — builds on existing components
Wow factor: High — refined and polished
Risk: Low — iterative improvement

B

Underground Dashboard

Transit control room aesthetic. The whole page feels like a metro operations dashboard. Hero has a flip-board displaying services. Projects appear as "arrivals." About is a bento control panel. Blog is a departure board. Rail becomes a data readout sidebar. More HUD/terminal vibes with JetBrains Mono everywhere.

Effort: Medium-High — mostly new components
Wow factor: High — unique concept
Risk: Medium — might feel too "techy"

C

Playful Metro Narrative

Most planetono-like. Scroll tells a story: you "enter" a metro station (portal effect), travel through themed environments. SQL station has flowing queries, Pipeline station has animated pipes, Analytics has floating charts. About is a "passenger profile." Blog is a departures board. Each station is its own mini-world with distinct visual personality.

Effort: Very High — custom animations per section
Wow factor: Maximum — awwwards territory
Risk: High — scope creep, performance concerns

All three share these sections:

1. Hero
2. Services (4)
3. Projects
4. About bento
5. Blog feed
6. CTA + contact
